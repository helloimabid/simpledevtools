Frequently Asked Questions

When should I use PNG vs JPG?

Use PNG for images that need transparency, logos, icons, screenshots, or graphics with sharp edges. Use JPG for photographs and complex images where small file size matters more than perfect quality.

What quality setting should I use?

For most uses, 85-92% quality offers a good balance. For web images, 80% is often sufficient. For print or archival, use 95-100%. Quality setting only affects JPG and WebP formats.

Why use WebP format?

WebP provides 25-35% smaller file sizes than JPG at equivalent quality, and supports transparency like PNG. It's ideal for websites to improve loading speed. All modern browsers support WebP.
